www.pathwaystosqcience.org is a Supercategories for field are - Medical & Life Sciences - Engineering & Physical Sciences - Mathematics & Computational Sciences - Earth, Environmental & Agriculture Sciences - Technology - Social Sciences - Business - Arts & Media You can also search by other factors such as region. This is a great resource for students interested in a Masters, PhD, or MD.   Advantages Summer research is great because it allows students to gain research experience while traveling and earning money. Programs typically pay for airfare, housing and a meal plan, while providing a a living stipend of around $5000. Programs that don't cover housing or meals typically pay more, e.g. $6000-$7000. Attending these programs shows prospective graduate schools and employers that the student has initiative. I have seen many students leave these internships with publications, making them much desirable for graduate school. If a student attends an elite institution such as MIT or Berkeley (as I'm sure many students in this program can and will want to do ;) ), they may have a chance to work with world-renowned professors that will help to launch their careers. All that aside, these programs are a great way to travel an explore the world while strengthening one's portfolio. Programs are both domestic and international. While students may be working during the week, they will have a chance to explore what ever city/state/country they are in on weekends. Applying For summer research, many of the more elite programs have deadlines in December and January. They also sometimes require lengthy essays (my application to Caltech required 4 essays and multiple free responses). For fellowships, many applications are due October/November. I believe my NSF application was due early October. I think the earlier students have access to information on these programs, the better they can prepare for them. Students will want to begin their applications (drafting essays, requesting letters of recommendation, etc.) 1-2 months in advance.Freshman, don't think that because you're a freshman that you can't get in! I met freshman that were accepted in to Caltech's program. Just write great essays!
