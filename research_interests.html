I like to think and theorize about how the brain may function

Method: Computer Science

I want to use computer science to further our understanding of the theoretical framework which underlies how the brain represents information and operates on it, i.e. how is information represented by electriic signals working in concert? I am also interested in the utility of computer science in data-driven analysis of how behavior is manifest of information being processed. The first is a bit low-level, the second a bit high-level--I like their marriage.

Approach: quasi-reductionist

I hope this isn't too contentious but I'm slightly reductionist--I believe that complex phenomena can be decomposed into more basic phenomena: I assume that there exist modular algorithms which underlie more complex algorithms. I hope that learning computer science theory and how computer science methods are being used to study the brain and simulate intelligence will provide me insight into what these algorithms may be.

Summary

I am interested in data-driven research coupled with research intended to abstract behavioral and information processing patterns into algorithms.
